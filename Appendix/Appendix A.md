## A.1 Nature of This Document

This book functions as a phenomenological systems specification.

It describes:

- observable experiential mechanisms  
- regulation dynamics  
- load formation  
- integration processes  

from first-person investigation.

It does not prescribe:

- standardized interventions  
- fixed curricula  
- or uniform training sequences.

---

## A.2 Appropriate Applications

This framework may inform:

- contemplative training  
- psychotherapy  
- somatic work  
- education  
- performance coaching  
- leadership development  
- trauma integration  
- curriculum design  

when used by qualified practitioners.

---

## A.3 Constraint-Based Design

Practices derived from this framework should respect:

1. Resolution precedes control  
2. Regulation precedes insight  
3. Discharge precedes stability  
4. Safety precedes intensity  
5. Autonomy precedes authority  

Violation of these constraints predicts adverse outcomes.

---

## A.4 Risk Conditions

High-risk contexts include:

- unresolved trauma  
- dissociation  
- psychosis history  
- active addiction  
- severe depression  
- unstable environments  

In these contexts, unsupervised application is contraindicated.

---

## A.5 Ethical Responsibility

Implementers retain full responsibility for:

- participant welfare  
- scope of practice  
- referral thresholds  
- informed consent  
- professional boundaries  

No part of this text constitutes medical, psychological, or legal advice.

---

## A.6 Non-Authoritative Status

This framework is descriptive, not doctrinal.

It does not claim ultimate truth.

It does not establish hierarchy.

It invites verification.
