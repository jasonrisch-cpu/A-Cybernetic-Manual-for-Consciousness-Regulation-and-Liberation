## Foundational Exercise: Locating the Cursor

A simple practice reveals the mechanism.

### Step 1 — Whole Hand

Without looking, feel your left hand.

Do not move it.

Notice:

- warmth  
- tingling  
- pressure  
- presence  

The hand “lights up.”

This is attention arriving.

---

### Step 2 — Fingertip

Now narrow to the fingertips.

Then to one fingertip.

Resolution increases.  
Noise decreases.  
Precision improves.

This is zoom.

---

### Step 3 — Cross-Body Shift

Now move attention to the right hand.

Most people notice:

- a sweep  
- a drag  
- a redistribution  

Not a jump.

This is attention in motion.

It has continuity.

---

### Step 4 — Return to Center

Bring attention back to the midline.

Notice:

- settling  
- balance  
- symmetry  

This becomes a reference position.

---

## What This Exercise Teaches

Without theory, it demonstrates:

- attention reorganizes sensation

Belief is no longer required.

The interface is directly observable.
